About UDAAN Program: Nurturing Future Civil Servants

Empowering Young Minds for Competitive Success


UDAAN Program has been sketched by Dhyeya IAS to accommodate the young minds who have completed their class 12th and are headed towards graduation level. This program has been in place for students aspiring to build a career as a Civil servant. The UDAAN program was launched by Dhyeya IAS with an aim to give wings to the dreams of young & strong minds, who are willing to put in the work and effort required to make their aspirations a reality. Team UDAAN understands the challenges associated with the preparation for competitive examination, the vast syllabus, the peer pressure etc. The UDAAN Program was started to tackle these problems at the very root of it.

The methodology followed by UDAAN Program provides guidance and mentorship to students paving way for them to clear exams like civil services and many more. Under the guidance of experienced and expert faculty UDAAN program provides comprehensive course curriculum equipping students to clear competitive exams like UPSC-CSE, PCS, EPFO etc. This program facilitates such young aspirants who carry a lot of zeal & willpower, to start preparing at a young age & achieve an advantageous position to crack the exam in the very first attempt itself, after their graduation.

Our Mission

Guiding Young Minds with Knowledge and Direction for a Bright Future


The 3-year UDAAN program powered by Dhyeya IAS was made with a mission to design a program accommodating young minds fresh out of school. This program aims to provide perspective to the students so that their energy can be channelized in the right direction. We want to equip students with right tool of knowledge and guidance to face the competition deciding their fate. Team UDAAN wants the young minds to realize their actual potential so that they can be more focused towards their career and face the competition fiercely.

Comprehensive UDAAN Course Structure

Building a Strong Foundation
for Future Civil Servants

UDAAN 1st YEAR- FOUNDATION LEVEL

In the first year we focus on developing the reading & analytical abilities of the students. We begin with teaching the basic subjects like geography, history, economy, polity etc with special focus on NCERT. We are dedicated towards simplification of the subjects & conceptual clarity which helps the aspirant develop better understanding resulting in development of critical analogy specially required for answer writing. In this year we aim to instil the competitive spirit in the students. We aim at developing the overall personality for which we conduct different activities in class.

UDAAN 2nd YEAR - TAKE OFF LEVEL

After the successful completion of the foundation level, we move onto the next stage of the course program. Our team follows a multi-disciplinary approach during this stage focusing on study assignments and General studies. Going through the 2nd year of the Udaan Program, our curriculum is focused on mentoring students to develop competency at the CSAT. The skills developed in 1st year and second year both will equip the students for CSAT, which will expand their horizons of appearing and clearing other exams CDS, PCS ,Assistant Commandant Exam etc.

UDAAN 3rd YEAR - FOCUSED LEVEL

The Udaan program reaches its final stage in the third year, the students are put through various tests and interviews in order to develop a favourable personality required to face and clear the prestigious exam like Civil Service Examination. During the final year we have also included in our curriculum to conduct counselling sessions with the experts so that the students can decide upon the optional subjects for the UPSC examination. Our team guides & mentors’ students thoroughly driving them towards success.

What is the UPSC Civil Services Examination (CSE)?

The UPSC CSE is a national-level competitive exam conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) to recruit candidates for top government positions like IAS, IPS, IFS, and other civil services.

What are the stages of the UPSC Civil Services Examination?

The UPSC CSE has three stages:
  • Preliminary Examination (Prelims): Objective type, with two papers: General Studies (GS) Paper 1 and Civil Services Aptitude Test (CSAT) Paper 2.
  • Main Examination (Mains): Descriptive type, consisting of 9 papers (2 qualifying, 7 considered for merit).
  • Personality Test (Interview): A final stage to assess the candidate’s suitability for the civil services.

How should I start preparing for UPSC?

  • Understand the syllabus: Begin by thoroughly understanding the syllabus for Prelims and Mains.
  • Read NCERT books: Cover subjects like history, geography, and polity using NCERT textbooks (Class 6-12).
  • Daily newspaper reading: Develop a habit of reading newspapers (e.g., The Hindu, Indian Express) and following current affairs.
  • Choose your optional subject: Select an optional subject that aligns with your strengths or interest for the Mains exam.
  • Make a study plan: Create a timetable that covers the entire syllabus with a mix of static and dynamic subjects.
  • Join coaching or study independently: Based on your comfort level, either enroll in coaching or use online resources for self-study.

What is the eligibility criteria for UPSC CSE?

  • Age limit: The candidate must be between 21 to 32 years (relaxation for reserved categories).
  • Educational qualification: A candidate must hold a degree from a recognized university.
  • Number of attempts: General category candidates have 6 attempts, OBC 9 attempts, while SC/ST candidates have unlimited attempts till the age limit.

What is the Prelims exam pattern?

  • Paper I (General Studies): 200 marks, 100 questions, duration 2 hours.
  • Paper II (CSAT): 200 marks, 80 questions, duration 2 hours (qualifying with 33% marks required).
  • Both papers are objective-type with negative marking for incorrect answers.

What is the Mains exam pattern?

The Mains consists of 9 papers (total 1750 marks). Papers include:
  • Essay (Paper I) – 250 marks.
  • General Studies (Papers II to V) – 250 marks each.
  • Optional Subject (Papers VI and VII) – 250 marks each.
  • Qualifying papers – English and one Indian language, each of 300 marks (only qualifying, not counted in merit).

How should I prepare for the Prelims?

  • Cover basic books: Read NCERTs, and other standard books (e.g., Laxmikanth for Polity, Spectrum for History).
  • Practice mock tests: Solve as many practice tests as possible to improve speed and accuracy.
  • Current affairs: Focus on current events of national and international importance.

How should I prepare for the Mains?

  • Answer writing practice: Regularly write answers to previous years' questions and model questions.
  • Revise regularly: Revision is crucial, especially for subjects like General Studies and the Optional paper.
  • Interlink static and dynamic topics: Learn how to incorporate current events with static syllabus topics.

What is the role of the interview in the selection process?

The interview is a personality test and carries 275 marks. It assesses qualities like communication skills, logical reasoning, clarity of thought, decision-making, and knowledge of current affairs. It is important to stay calm and confident while answering questions during the interview.

Is coaching necessary for UPSC preparation?

Coaching is not mandatory. Many aspirants have cleared UPSC without coaching. However, it can provide structure and guidance for those who need it. Online resources and self-study are equally effective if done consistently.

How much time is needed for UPSC preparation?

On average, a candidate requires 1-2 years of consistent preparation to cover the entire syllabus and revise adequately.

How important is the selection of optional subject?

The optional subject carries 500 marks in the Mains exam, so it plays a crucial role in your final ranking. Choose a subject that aligns with your interest, academic background, or overlaps with the General Studies syllabus.

What are the best books for UPSC preparation?

  • Indian Polity by Laxmikanth.
  • History of Modern India by Spectrum.
  • Indian Economy by Ramesh Singh.
  • Environment by Shankar IAS.
  • NCERTs (Class 6-12).
  • Current Affairs (e.g., Yojana, PIB, Newspapers).

How important is current affairs in UPSC?

Current affairs are crucial for both Prelims and Mains. Many questions are based on recent events and developments. Candidates should follow news, government schemes, and editorials regularly.

Can working professionals prepare for UPSC?

Yes, many working professionals prepare for UPSC. Proper time management and smart study techniques are key. Evening and weekend study sessions can help balance work and preparation.

What is the importance of answer writing practice?

Answer writing is essential for Mains, as it helps improve clarity, presentation, and time management during the exam. Practice helps in structuring answers and conveying ideas concisely.

How do I handle failure in the UPSC exam?

It’s important to learn from mistakes, analyze what went wrong (e.g., lack of practice, insufficient revision), and stay motivated. UPSC requires perseverance, and many successful candidates have failed multiple times before clearing the exam.

What are the common mistakes to avoid in UPSC preparation?

  • Skipping the syllabus: Not referring to the UPSC syllabus regularly.
  • Neglecting revision: Focusing only on reading new material without revising old topics.
  • Ignoring answer writing practice: Waiting until the last moment to practice writing for Mains.
  • Neglecting CSAT: Failing to practice for CSAT despite it being a qualifying paper.
